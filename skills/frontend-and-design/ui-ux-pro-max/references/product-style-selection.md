# Product Style Selection

Choose style based on product job, trust profile, and pace of use.

## Conservative styles

Use for:

- fintech
- healthcare
- enterprise operations
- admin surfaces
- B2B workflows with dense information

Characteristics:

- restrained color accents
- high legibility
- predictable layout
- low ornament
- strong spacing and hierarchy

Good fit when the product must feel trustworthy, calm, and operational.

## Expressive styles

Use for:

- creative tools
- consumer apps
- portfolio sites
- lifestyle brands
- marketing surfaces with strong storytelling

Characteristics:

- more daring typography
- stronger art direction
- more contrast in scale and composition
- selective motion and texture

Good fit when the product must feel memorable, premium, or emotionally charged.

## Technical-product styles

Use for:

- developer tools
- AI products
- analytics platforms
- infrastructure products

Characteristics:

- crisp type
- clear density rules
- data-friendly spacing
- strong dark/light theme logic
- interfaces that reward repeated use

Good fit when the product needs capability and precision more than warmth.

## Style selection questions

Before choosing a direction, answer:

- Is this product trust-heavy or excitement-heavy?
- Will people use it once in a while or for hours at a time?
- Does the interface need to disappear or persuade?
- Is the primary surface narrative, operational, or exploratory?

## Practical mapping

- Landing page for a new brand: push farther on typography and composition.
- Dashboard with repeat users: reduce decoration and invest in hierarchy.
- Mobile utility flow: bias toward clarity, tap comfort, and short paths.
- Settings/admin surface: prioritize density discipline and predictable states.

## Warning signs

- A dashboard using a marketing-site visual language.
- A healthcare or fintech surface with playful low-contrast styling.
- A landing page that looks like a default admin template.
- A complex product trying to express identity only through gradients.
